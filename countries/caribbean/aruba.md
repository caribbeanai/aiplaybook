## Sector 1: Tourism

*Aruba draws over 1 million stopover visitors and 900,000+ cruise passengers annually, primarily from the USA, Netherlands, and Venezuelan diaspora. Eagle Beach is consistently rated among the world's best. The Palm Beach hotel strip anchors the mass-market tourism offer. The cruise port at Oranjestad is one of the busiest in the Caribbean.*

### Use Case 1: Hotel and Resort Revenue Optimisation

**Role**: Revenue Manager, Hotel General Manager
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
Create a revenue optimisation strategy for a hotel or resort in Aruba.
- Property name and location: [Palm Beach strip / Eagle Beach / San Nicolas]
- Property tier: [luxury resort / all-inclusive / boutique / timeshare]
- Room count and room categories: [describe]
- Primary feeder markets: [USA northeast / Midwest / Netherlands / Venezuelan diaspora / Colombia]
- Peak vs. shoulder season: [high season Jan–April, low season Sep–Nov]
- Current average daily rate (ADR): [AWG or USD]
- Occupancy challenge: [describe current occupancy rates and gaps]
- Competing properties: [name 2–3 direct competitors on Palm Beach]
- Distribution channels: [OTA / direct / wholesale / travel agent]

Produce: dynamic pricing strategy by season and market,
direct booking campaign concept with incentives,
upsell package ideas (Eagle Beach sunset experience / aloe spa day / Aruba food tour),
Venezuelan and Colombian market outreach plan (Spanish/Papiamento messaging),
group and MICE sales strategy,
and loyalty programme framework for repeat Aruba visitors.
```
**Benefits**: Increases ADR and occupancy by aligning pricing to market demand segments and closing the shoulder-season revenue gap.

## Sector 2: Oil Refining

*The Valero refinery in San Nicolas (formerly the LAGO Oil and Transport Company, founded 1924) is one of the largest in the Caribbean. Its reactivation discussions and CITGO historical connections make the refinery sector central to Aruba's economic diversification debate. The San Nicolas waterfront is being redeveloped as a cultural and tourism hub alongside the industrial zone.*

### Use Case 3: Refinery Economic Impact and Stakeholder Communication

**Role**: Minister of Economic Affairs, Aruba Refinery Communications Manager
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
Prepare an economic impact analysis and stakeholder communication package for the Aruba oil refinery sector.
- Refinery location: San Nicolas, south-east Aruba
- Current operational status: [active / partial capacity / under renegotiation]
- Refining capacity: [describe barrels per day if known]
- Direct employment: [estimated number of Aruban workers]
- Indirect economic impact: [supply chain, San Nicolas community, government revenue]
- Environmental obligations: [emission standards, SETAR energy linkage, carbon transition]
- Stakeholder groups: [Aruban government, community of San Nicolas, labor unions, Netherlands Kingdom partners]
- Geopolitical context: [Venezuela oil supply, CITGO history, US sanctions considerations]

Produce: economic impact summary for government and public,
labor union engagement briefing,
environmental compliance communication,
San Nicolas community development integration narrative,
media Q&A on refinery reactivation,
and energy transition scenario analysis (refinery vs. renewables).
```
**Benefits**: Enables evidence-based public dialogue on refinery operations, balancing economic necessity with environmental and community considerations.

## Sector 3: Aloe Vera

*Aruba Aloe (founded 1890) is one of the world's oldest and most respected aloe vera brands, producing skincare, cosmetics, and pharmaceutical-grade aloe products from Aruba's native Aloe barbadensis miller plants. Sustainable, wind-powered production and export to cosmetics and pharma markets worldwide make this a flagship Aruban industry.*

### Use Case 4: Aloe Vera Export and Brand Storytelling

**Role**: Aruba Aloe Export Manager, Brand Marketing Director
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
Create an export strategy and brand storytelling package for Aruba aloe vera products.
- Brand: [Aruba Aloe or independent producer]
- Product categories: [raw aloe gel / skincare / suncare / pharmaceuticals / nutraceuticals]
- Target export markets: [USA / Netherlands / Germany / Japan / Brazil]
- Sustainability certifications held: [organic / cruelty-free / ISO / fair trade]
- Production method differentiator: [wind-powered, hand-harvested Aruban aloe since 1890]
- Retail channel target: [luxury department stores / pharmacies / e-commerce / spa wholesale]
- Regulatory requirements in target market: [FDA / EU Cosmetics Regulation / ANVISA]
- Papiamento/Dutch brand heritage angle: [describe cultural story]

Produce: brand origin story (250 words, premium tone),
export market entry brief for [target country],
trade show pitch deck outline (Cosmoprof / Natural Products Expo),
e-commerce product description set (5 SKUs),
sustainability credentials one-pager,
retailer pitch email,
and social media content celebrating Aruban aloe heritage.
```
**Benefits**: Positions Aruba aloe as a premium, sustainably sourced ingredient in global cosmetics and wellness markets, driving export revenue growth.
