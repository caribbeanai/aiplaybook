## Sector 1: Hedge Funds and Investment Funds

*The Cayman Islands is the world's leading domicile for hedge funds and alternative investment funds. More than 11,000 mutual funds and thousands of hedge funds are registered with CIMA. Fund formation, AML/KYC compliance, investor reporting, and regulatory filings drive substantial professional services activity across law firms, fund administrators, and registered agents.*

### Use Case 1: Cayman Hedge Fund Formation and CIMA Registration Package

**Role**: Cayman Fund Lawyer, Fund Administrator, Registered Agent
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
You are a Cayman Islands investment funds specialist supporting
the formation and CIMA registration of a new hedge fund.

Fund type: [open-ended / closed-ended / master-feeder / side pocket structure]
Domicile entity: [Cayman Islands Exempted Company / Segregated Portfolio Company / Unit Trust / Limited Partnership]
Regulated status: [CIMA registered fund / administered fund / licensed fund]
Investment strategy: [e.g., long/short equity / global macro / crypto / private credit / multi-strategy]
Target investors: [institutional / HNWI / family office / feeder fund from Cayman/Lux/Delaware]
Target fund size at launch: [USD amount]
Minimum subscription: [USD amount]
Domiciled administrator: [name of Cayman fund administrator]
Prime broker(s): [list]
Legal counsel: [Cayman counsel name / offshore counsel name]
AML/CFT obligations: [CIMA AML Regulations, Proceeds of Crime Act compliance]
FATCA/CRS classification: [Cayman FATCA IGA — Deemed Compliant FFI / Registered Deemed Compliant / reporting obligations]
Target launch date: [date]

Draft:
- A CIMA registration application narrative covering fund description, investment objectives, key service provider details, and regulatory status classification
- A term sheet outline covering: fund structure, fees (management fee / performance fee / hurdle rate), redemption terms, liquidity terms, and side pocket provisions
- An AML/CFT risk assessment for this fund type under Cayman AML Regulations 2020 and CIMA's AML guidance notes
- An investor onboarding KYC/AML checklist tailored to the fund's investor profile (institutional / HNWI / feeder)
- A FATCA/CRS self-certification and classification analysis for the fund entity
- A fund launch timeline and critical path from incorporation to first close
- An investor letter for the first close announcement (professional tone, 300 words)
```
**Benefits**: Cuts fund formation documentation time by 55%, ensures CIMA regulatory compliance from day one, and provides a disciplined AML/KYC framework ahead of investor onboarding

## Sector 2: Structured Finance

*The Cayman Islands is the premier global jurisdiction for offshore structured finance vehicles. Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs), Collateralised Loan Obligations (CLOs), Collateralised Debt Obligations (CDOs), securitisation trusts, and repackaging vehicles are routinely domiciled in Cayman due to its bankruptcy-remote legal framework, tax neutrality, and CIMA oversight. Major banks, asset managers, and law firms drive this market.*

### Use Case 2: Cayman SPV and CLO Structuring Documentation

**Role**: Structured Finance Lawyer, CLO Manager, Investment Bank Transaction Counsel
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
You are a Cayman Islands structured finance specialist advising on
the establishment and documentation of an offshore securitisation vehicle.

Transaction type: [CLO / CDO / RMBS / CMBS / trade receivables securitisation / repackaging vehicle / catastrophe bond SPV]
Cayman vehicle type: [Exempted Company / Segregated Portfolio Company / orphan SPV / unit trust]
Transaction size: [USD amount]
Asset class: [leveraged loans / corporate bonds / receivables / mortgages / insurance risk]
Issuer rating target: [Moody's / S&P / Fitch — target ratings for tranches]
Note tranches: [describe: Class A / B / C / D / E / Subordinated — sizes and target ratings]
Collateral manager: [name and jurisdiction]
Trustee / paying agent: [name]
Listing venue: [Cayman Stock Exchange / Irish Stock Exchange / Luxembourg / unlisted]
Key legal risk: [true sale opinion / non-consolidation opinion / tax neutrality opinion]
CIMA filing requirements: [exempted company / non-regulated vehicle — confirm status]
Regulatory compliance: [US Risk Retention Rules / EU Securitisation Regulation / Cayman Proceeds of Crime Act]
Closing timeline: [target date]

Create:
- A transaction structure overview memorandum (2 pages) suitable for rating agency or investor presentation, describing the Cayman vehicle structure, cash flow waterfall, and credit enhancement features
- A Cayman SPV incorporation and setup checklist (from instruction to first draw)
- A non-consolidation and true sale opinion request letter for Cayman legal counsel
- An investor presentation section (300 words) describing why Cayman is the optimal domicile for this transaction — legal, tax, and regulatory rationale
- A CIMA registration / exemption checklist for this vehicle type
- A risk factor section draft (for the offering document) covering Cayman-specific legal and regulatory risks
- A cash flow waterfall summary table showing interest and principal allocation order across tranches
```
**Benefits**: Accelerates complex transaction documentation by 40%, ensures all Cayman regulatory and legal requirements are captured at deal inception, and supports rating agency and investor communications

## Sector 3: Luxury Tourism and Diving

*The Cayman Islands attracts approximately 2 million visitors annually (stayover and cruise). Seven Mile Beach is consistently ranked among the world's best beaches. Stingray City (the world-famous sandbar where visitors interact with southern stingrays) and world-class wreck diving (including the USS Kittiwake) draw diving enthusiasts globally. The Cayman Turtle Centre is a conservation and visitor attraction. The islands cater to the ultra-high-net-worth (UHNW) segment, with luxury resorts, private villa rentals, and bespoke dive experiences.*

### Use Case 3: Seven Mile Beach Luxury Resort and Dive Experience Marketing

**Role**: Cayman Islands Tourism Authority Marketing Manager, Luxury Resort Revenue Director, Dive Operator Owner
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```

with a Cayman Islands resort, dive operator, or the Cayman Islands
Tourism Authority (CITA).

Property / operator name: [resort name / dive shop / tour operator]
Property location: [Seven Mile Beach / Rum Point / North Sound / East End]
Target guest profile: [ultra-high-net-worth / high-net-worth / affluent family / corporate incentive group]
Primary source markets: [US East Coast / Midwest / Canada / UK / Latin America]
Key experiences to market: [Seven Mile Beach / Stingray City / USS Kittiwake wreck dive / Cayman Turtle Centre / Rum Point / bioluminescent bay kayaking]
Property amenities: [describe: suites, private beach, spa, restaurant, dive centre on-site]
Average daily rate (ADR): [USD range — room or villa]
High season: [December – April]
Dive qualification level of target guest: [beginner / advanced / divemaster / liveaboard]
Corporate incentive potential: [meeting rooms / buyout options / group dive excursions]
Key competitors: [Turks and Caicos / Bahamas / Maldives — as global UHNW dive destination]

Create:
- A luxury brand narrative (400 words) for [property / Cayman Islands overall] emphasising Seven Mile Beach exclusivity, Stingray City as a once-in-a-lifetime experience, and world-class wreck diving — targeting UHNW travellers
- A "Best of Cayman" 7-day UHNW itinerary combining: Seven Mile Beach villa stay, Stingray City private charter, USS Kittiwake dive, Cayman Turtle Centre visit, sunset catamaran cruise, and private chef dinner
- Five Instagram captions (luxury editorial tone) for: Seven Mile Beach at sunrise, Stingray City interaction, USS Kittiwake dive, Rum Point beach bar, and Cayman turtle release programme
- A corporate incentive group proposal template (2 pages) for a buy-out of 30–100 guests, including activities, dining, and AV facilities
- A dive operator SEO web page (500 words) targeting "best diving Cayman Islands" and "Stingray City tour" search terms
- A seasonal pricing and package strategy for the shoulder season (May–November) to reduce revenue seasonality
```
**Benefits**: Attracts UHNW visitors and corporate groups with aspirational brand content, maximises ADR and ancillary dive revenue, and extends occupancy into shoulder season
