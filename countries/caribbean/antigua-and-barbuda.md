## Sector 1: Citizenship by Investment (CBI)
*Antigua and Barbuda's Citizenship by Investment Programme, administered by the Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU), is one of the Caribbean's most well-established and globally respected CBI programmes. Investment options include a real estate route, National Development Fund (NDF) contribution, University of the West Indies (UWI) Fund, and a business investment route. The programme attracts high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs) primarily from the Middle East, Asia, Africa, and Eastern Europe seeking visa-free travel, estate planning flexibility, and tax neutrality.*

### Use Case 1: CBI Application Due Diligence and Client Advisory
**Role**: CBI Licensed Agent, Immigration Lawyer, Family Office Advisor, Authorised Promoter
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
Draft a comprehensive CBI advisory and due diligence brief for
[licensed agent firm / law firm] advising a prospective applicant
for Antigua and Barbuda citizenship.

Client profile: [individual / family — number of dependants].
Client nationality: [country].
Client background: [business owner / investor / professional].
Investment route preferred: [NDF contribution / real estate / UWI Fund / business investment].
Investment budget: [USD amount].
Purpose: [visa-free travel / estate planning / tax residency / second passport / education access].
Key visa-free destinations required: [UK / EU Schengen / UAE / USA (ESTA eligibility note)].
Current passport(s) held: [list].
Timeline: [desired processing timeframe].
Risk profile: [any PEP / adverse media / complex source of funds considerations].

Produce:
- summary of Antigua and Barbuda CBI Programme routes with current investment thresholds,
- eligibility assessment for the client profile,
- due diligence document checklist (CIU requirements: police clearance, medical, financials, source of funds),
- source of funds narrative and documentation framework,
- real estate route shortlist (approved projects in Antigua — examples: Nonsuch Bay, Half Moon Bay, Pearns Point),
- NDF vs. real estate route cost-benefit comparison for this client,
- timeline from application to passport issuance (currently approximately 3–6 months),
- family unit inclusion strategy (spouse, children, parents, siblings),
- estate planning integration (trust / foundation overlay on Antigua citizenship),
- and post-citizenship services checklist (tax residency, banking, travel document renewal).
```
**Benefits**: Enables licensed CBI agents and lawyers to deliver thorough, compliant, and persuasive advisory services to HNWI clients, reducing processing delays, improving due diligence quality, and increasing client confidence in the Antigua CBI programme.

## Sector 2: Luxury Tourism and Yachting
*Antigua and Barbuda is one of the Eastern Caribbean's premier luxury destinations, renowned for Antigua Sailing Week (one of the world's top ten regattas), its 365 beaches, superyacht marina infrastructure at Falmouth Harbour and English Harbour, and a growing inventory of ultra-luxury resorts (Jumby Bay, Carlisle Bay, The Inn at English Harbour). Barbuda offers an untouched counterpoint — low-impact luxury eco-tourism post-Hurricane Irma reconstruction.*

### Use Case 2: Superyacht Charter and Marina Services Marketing
**Role**: Marina Manager, Yacht Charter Broker, Falmouth Harbour Marinas / English Harbour Operations, Luxury Charter Agent
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
Develop a superyacht charter marketing and marina services strategy
for [marina / charter company / yacht services provider] in Antigua.

Business type: [marina operator / charter broker / provisioning / technical services / crew agency].
Marina location: [Falmouth Harbour / English Harbour / Jolly Harbour / other].
Target vessel size: [LOA range — 30m / 50m / 80m / 100m+].
Client profile: [charter clients / yacht owners / delivery crews / racing teams].
Key events to target: [Antigua Sailing Week / Superyacht Challenge / Caribbean charter season Dec–Apr].
Current berth capacity: [number of slips, max LOA].
Current annual revenue: [USD amount or "confidential"].
Competing marinas: [Gustavia St Barths / Simpson Bay St Maarten / Rodney Bay St Lucia].
Services offered: [fuel / provisions / crew / technical / customs clearance].
Distribution: [broker relationships / direct / charter platforms].

Create:
- luxury superyacht marketing narrative (English, French for Francophone charter market),
- Antigua Sailing Week sponsorship and activation strategy,
- digital marketing plan (Yacht Charter Fleet, Burgess, Fraser, YachtCharterFleet, Instagram),
- superyacht provisioning and concierge service package,
- technical services marketing (refit, engineering, electronics, rigging),
- crew recruitment and training programme in partnership with Antigua Cadet School,
- comparison positioning vs. St Barths, St Maarten, and St Lucia marina offer,
- pricing strategy for peak season (December–April) and off-season retention,
- customer journey from enquiry to departure (concierge touchpoints),
- and partnership strategy with private jet operators and Antigua's luxury hotels.
```
**Benefits**: Maximises revenue per berth, positions Antigua marinas competitively against French West Indies rivals, extends yacht stays, and grows the high-yield, low-volume superyacht economy.

## Sector 3: Financial Services — Offshore Banking, IBC, and Asset Protection
*Antigua and Barbuda is a recognised offshore financial services jurisdiction regulated by the Financial Services Regulatory Commission (FSRC). The country offers International Business Corporations (IBCs), offshore banking licences, trust and private foundation structures, and an emerging digital assets regulatory framework. As a common law jurisdiction and CARICOM member, Antigua is an attractive location for asset protection and family wealth structuring.*

### Use Case 3: Offshore Asset Protection Structure Design
**Role**: FSRC-Licensed Financial Services Provider, Trust Company, Law Firm, Family Office
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
Draft an asset protection and wealth structuring advisory for
[trust company / law firm / financial services firm] in Antigua and Barbuda.

Client profile: [HNWI / family office / entrepreneur / estate].
Client domicile: [country of residence and citizenship].
Assets to protect: [real estate / business interests / investment portfolio / liquid assets — USD value range].
Threats to address: [litigation risk / divorce / creditor claims / political risk / succession].
Structures available in Antigua: [IBC / Trust / Foundation / Private Interest Foundation].
Desired outcomes: [asset protection / confidentiality / succession planning / tax neutrality].
Reporting obligations: [CRS / FATCA / local beneficial ownership registry].
Banking requirements: [Antigua bank / international correspondent].
Other jurisdictions in scope: [BVI / Cayman / Nevis / St Kitts / other].

Produce:
- recommended asset protection structure (IBC + Trust or Foundation overlay),
- Antigua IBC incorporation and trust registration process and costs,
- comparison of Antigua vs. Nevis / BVI / Cayman for the stated objective,
- beneficial ownership compliance (Antigua's beneficial ownership framework — FSRC requirements),
- CRS/FATCA reporting obligations and mitigation strategies,
- trustee and protector appointment recommendations,
- banking solutions compatible with Antigua-based structures (CIBC FirstCaribbean, Eastern Caribbean Amalgamated Bank, offshore banking partners),
- succession planning integration (Letter of Wishes, protector mechanism),
- annual compliance calendar (FSRC reporting, IBC annual fees, trust review),
- and digital assets holding considerations under Antigua's emerging DABA framework.
```
**Benefits**: Enables Antigua-based financial services providers to deliver sophisticated, compliant asset protection advisory to HNWI clients, strengthening the jurisdiction's competitive position against BVI and Nevis while meeting evolving OECD/FATF transparency standards.
