## Sector 1: Pharmaceutical Manufacturing / Manufactura Farmacéutica

*Puerto Rico hosts the world's largest concentration of FDA-approved pharmaceutical manufacturing plants. Companies including Pfizer, Johnson & Johnson, AstraZeneca, Amgen, and Bristol Myers Squibb operate on the island. Pharmaceuticals account for more than 50% of Puerto Rico's exports. Current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) compliance, FDA inspections, and Act 60 R&D incentives are critical business drivers.*

*Puerto Rico alberga la mayor concentración de plantas farmacéuticas aprobadas por la FDA en el mundo. Empresas como Pfizer, Johnson & Johnson y AstraZeneca operan en la isla. Los productos farmacéuticos representan más del 50% de las exportaciones de Puerto Rico.*

### Use Case 1: FDA cGMP Compliance Documentation and Inspection Readiness / Documentación de Cumplimiento FDA cGMP y Preparación para Inspecciones

**Role**: Regulatory Affairs Manager, Quality Assurance Director, cGMP Compliance Officer
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
You are an FDA regulatory affairs and cGMP compliance specialist
supporting a pharmaceutical manufacturing facility in Puerto Rico.

Facility name and location: [e.g., Pfizer Vega Baja / J&J Gurabo / AstraZeneca Barceloneta]
Product type manufactured: [small molecule API / biologics / injectables / solid oral dose]
FDA inspection type anticipated: [routine GMP / pre-approval / for-cause]
Most recent FDA Form 483 observations (if any): [list observations]
Current SOPs under review: [list affected SOPs — e.g., cleaning validation, equipment qualification]
Batch record in question: [batch number, product, manufacturing date]
Regulatory submission type: [NDA / ANDA / BLA / supplement]
Act 60 R&D tax credit activities at this facility: [describe qualifying R&D]
LUMA Energy / industrial power reliability considerations: [any power-related GMP risk mitigation]
Language preference for internal SOPs: [English / Spanish / bilingual]

Draft:
- An FDA inspection readiness checklist covering: document control, equipment logs, personnel training records, environmental monitoring, and corrective/preventive action (CAPA) status
- A CAPA response template for a specific Form 483 observation (insert observation text above)
- A cGMP SOP section for [cleaning validation / computer system validation / batch record review]
- An Act 60 R&D tax incentive activity documentation narrative for Puerto Rico DDEC (Department of Economic Development and Commerce) filing
- An executive briefing (bilingual English/Spanish) summarising inspection readiness status for plant leadership
- A risk assessment matrix for the top 5 GMP compliance risks at this facility
```
**Benefits**: Reduces inspection preparation time by 50%, ensures audit-ready documentation at all times, and captures Act 60 R&D tax credit value accurately

## Sector 2: Act 60 Tax Incentives / Incentivos de la Ley 60

*Act 60 of 2019 (Puerto Rico Incentives Code) consolidates Puerto Rico's tax incentive programs. Key provisions: 4% corporate tax for eligible export services businesses, 0% tax on dividends and capital gains for individual investors who become bona fide residents. This has attracted thousands of US mainland businesses and high-net-worth individuals, including crypto investors, fund managers, and technology entrepreneurs.*

*La Ley 60 de 2019 (Código de Incentivos de Puerto Rico) ofrece una tasa corporativa del 4% para empresas de servicios de exportación y 0% en dividendos y ganancias de capital para residentes bona fide. Ha atraído a miles de empresas del continente y personas de alto patrimonio.*

### Use Case 2: Act 60 Decree Application and Export Services Business Setup / Solicitud de Decreto Ley 60 y Establecimiento de Negocio de Servicios de Exportación

**Role**: Act 60 Tax Attorney, Business Development Consultant, US Investor Relocating to Puerto Rico
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
You are a Puerto Rico Act 60 tax incentives specialist and business
formation advisor.

Applicant type: [US mainland individual investor / corporation relocating / new Puerto Rico entity]
Business activity: [export services type — e.g., fund management / consulting / technology services / crypto trading / insurance / legal services]
Annual revenue projected from eligible export services: [USD amount]
Individual bona fide residency plan: [183+ days / purchase of Puerto Rico home / charitable contribution commitment]
Target Act 60 decree chapter: [Chapter 2 — Export Services / Chapter 6 — Individual Investors]
DDEC filing timeline: [target date]
Puerto Rico CPA and attorney engaged: [yes/no — names if applicable]
Capital gains to shelter (for individual investors): [type: crypto / equities / fund interests — approximate USD amount]
Existing US C-Corp or LLC to restructure: [describe current structure]
Compliance obligations: [IRS Form 8898 bona fide residency / Puerto Rico Form 480 / Act 60 annual report]

Create:
- An Act 60 decree application narrative for Chapter [2 / 6] covering: business description, export services justification, economic contributions to Puerto Rico (jobs, salaries, local purchases)
- A step-by-step Act 60 compliance calendar (Year 1–3) covering DDEC filing, annual report, IRS Form 8898, bona fide residency requirements, and charitable contribution deadline
- A comparison table: Puerto Rico Act 60 vs. US mainland tax structure vs. other offshore jurisdictions (Cayman, BVI) — for client presentation
- A business plan outline for a qualifying Act 60 export services entity (technology / consulting / fund management)
- A Puerto Rico entity structure recommendation: PR LLC vs. PR Corporation vs. branch office
- A FAQ document (bilingual English/Spanish) for prospective Act 60 applicants addressing the most common questions about bona fide residency and the 4% rate
```
**Benefits**: Streamlines complex Act 60 applications, reduces legal preparation costs, and helps clients understand multi-year compliance obligations clearly

## Sector 3: Tourism / Turismo

*Puerto Rico receives approximately 5 million visitors annually. Key attractions include Old San Juan (UNESCO World Heritage candidate), the Bioluminescent Bay in Vieques (one of the brightest in the world), El Yunque National Rainforest (the only US National Forest in the Caribbean), and the island's vibrant food scene. San Juan is one of the Caribbean's busiest cruise ports.*

*Puerto Rico recibe aproximadamente 5 millones de visitantes al año. Las principales atracciones incluyen el Viejo San Juan, la Bahía Bioluminiscente de Vieques, El Yunque y una vibrante escena gastronómica. San Juan es uno de los puertos de cruceros más activos del Caribe.*

### Use Case 3: Bilingual Tourism Content Strategy and Destination Marketing / Estrategia de Contenido Turístico Bilingüe y Marketing de Destino

**Role**: Puerto Rico Tourism Company (PRTC) Marketing Officer, Boutique Hotel Owner, Tour Operator
**AI Tool**: Claude
**Prompt Structure**:
```
You are a bilingual (English/Spanish) destination marketing specialist
for Puerto Rico, working with the Puerto Rico Tourism Company (PRTC)
or a private tourism operator.

Property / operator name: [hotel name / tour company / experience operator]
Primary target markets: [US East Coast / European / Latin American visitors]
Key attractions to feature: [Old San Juan / Bioluminescent Bay Vieques / El Yunque / Rincon surfing / Ponce / Culebra beaches]
Seasonality: [high season Dec–April / hurricane recovery messaging if applicable]
Cruise arrivals context: [San Juan cruise terminal — volume and turnaround excursion opportunities]
Hurricane Maria resilience narrative: [how to address and reframe for international visitors]
Gastronomic tourism highlights: [mofongo / lechón de Bayamón / Old San Juan restaurants / food trucks]
Rum production tourism: [Bacardi Distillery tour / Casa Bacardi Catano / local craft rum brands]
Language split for content: [English for US market / Spanish for LatAm / bilingual for social media]
Act 60 lifestyle tourism angle: [attracting US Act 60 investors as long-stay visitors / residents]

Create:
- A 12-month bilingual (English/Spanish) tourism content calendar featuring seasonal highlights, festivals (San Sebastián Street Festival — January, Casals Festival, Fiestas Patronales), and culinary events
- A 7-day "La Isla del Encanto" visitor itinerary covering Old San Juan, El Yunque, Vieques Bioluminescent Bay, Rincon, and Ponce — in both English and Spanish
- Five bilingual Instagram captions (English + Spanish) for each of the top 5 Puerto Rico attractions
- A cruise passenger shore excursion package description (4-hour Old San Juan and mofongo lunch experience) for wholesale pitch to cruise lines
- A Hurricane Maria resilience and recovery press release for international travel media (English), positioning Puerto Rico as stronger and more vibrant than ever
- A bilingual email sequence (3 emails) for hotel guest pre-arrival, on-property, and post-stay follow-up
```
**Benefits**: Reaches both English and Spanish-speaking audiences with culturally resonant content, maximises cruise and stayover revenue, and reframes resilience as a competitive brand asset
