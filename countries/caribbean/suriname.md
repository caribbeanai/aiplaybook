## Sector 1: Oil & Gas — Offshore Suriname Block 58
*TotalEnergies and APA Corporation (Apache) operate Block 58, where a series of major oil discoveries since 2020 could make Suriname one of the Caribbean's leading oil producers within this decade.*

### Use Case 1: Block 58 Local Content Strategy
**Role**: Local Content Officer, SaramaccaEnergy Procurement Manager, Ministry of Natural Resources Advisor
**AI Tool / Herramienta de IA**: Claude
**Prompt Structure / Estructura del Prompt**:
```
Develop a Local Content compliance and development strategy for
[company name] operating under Block 58 Production Sharing Agreement
in Suriname's offshore oil sector.

Operator: TotalEnergies / APA Corp / [subcontractor name].
Reporting period: [quarter / year].
Surinamese nationals employed: [number, % of total workforce].
Surinamese vendors contracted: [list with SRD/USD contract values].
Training provided to Surinamese workers: [hours, courses, certifications].
Local procurement spend in SRD and USD: [amounts by category].
Regulatory framework: Staatsolie / Ministry of Natural Resources requirements.
Community investment: [Paramaribo / coastal district programmes].

Draft:
- Local Content compliance report for submission to Staatsolie and Ministry of Natural Resources,
- gap analysis against Suriname's Local Content Policy,
- Surinamese supplier development roadmap (capacity building, financing, certification),
- workforce localisation plan with 3-year targets,
- community benefit-sharing strategy for coastal fishing communities affected by offshore operations,
- recommended MOU with Anton de Kom University of Suriname for training programmes,
- and 5-year local content improvement plan benchmarked against Guyana's Local Content Act 2021.
```
**Benefits / Beneficios**: Ensures regulatory compliance, builds Surinamese capacity in the oil sector, reduces reputational risk, and positions local suppliers competitively before first oil production begins.

## Sector 2: Gold Mining — Large-Scale and Artisanal (ASGM)
*Gold mining accounts for more than 50% of Suriname's export earnings. Major operators include Newmont (Merian mine) and IAMGOLD (Rosebel mine), while tens of thousands of artisanal and small-scale gold miners (ASGM) — many undocumented — operate throughout the interior, often using mercury.*

### Use Case 2: Artisanal Gold Mining (ASGM) Mercury Reduction Programme
**Role**: Ministry of Natural Resources Officer, Staatsolie Environmental Manager, NGO Programme Coordinator, ASGM Community Leader
**AI Tool / Herramienta de IA**: Claude
**Prompt Structure / Estructura del Prompt**:
```
Design a mercury reduction and ASGM formalisation programme for
artisanal and small-scale gold miners in Suriname's interior.

Region / Maroon community area: [Brokopondo / Para / Sipaliwini district].
Estimated number of active ASGM miners: [number].
Current mercury use: [kg per week / estimated].
Suriname's Minamata Convention commitment: ratified [year].
Existing government programme: [Staatsolie / NIMOS involvement].
Maroon and indigenous community context: [Saramaka / Ndyuka / Trio peoples].
Available alternative technology: [BLEG / retort equipment / gravity methods].
International support available: [UNEP / GEF / IDB].

Develop:
- phased mercury elimination roadmap (Minamata Convention Article 7 compliant),
- ASGM miner formalisation pathway under Suriname's Mining Decree,
- alternative gold recovery technology adoption plan (retort, gravity concentration),
- community health programme for miners and families (mercury poisoning screening),
- economic alternative livelihoods for vulnerable miners (eco-tourism, sustainable agriculture),
- Maroon and indigenous community engagement and consent protocol (FPIC),
- monitoring and reporting system using satellite and mobile technology,
- and financing strategy (IDB / World Bank / Suriname government co-funding).
```
**Benefits / Beneficios**: Protects interior community health, reduces environmental contamination of Suriname's rivers and Amazon basin, improves export compliance, and positions Suriname to access premium ethical gold markets.

## Sector 3: Eco-Tourism — Amazon Rainforest and Nature Reserves
*Suriname contains one of the world's most pristine Amazonian rainforests, covering over 90% of its territory. Key assets include the Central Suriname Nature Reserve (UNESCO World Heritage Site), Raleighvallen Nature Reserve, and CELOS research station. Eco-tourism remains underdeveloped relative to its potential.*

### Use Case 3: Eco-Tourism Product Development — Central Suriname Nature Reserve
**Role**: Suriname Tourism Foundation (STF) Officer, Lodge Owner, Ministry of Regional Development Planner, CELOS Researcher
**AI Tool / Herramienta de IA**: Claude
**Prompt Structure / Estructura del Prompt**:
```
Develop an eco-tourism product and marketing strategy for
[lodge / tour operator / government agency] operating in or near
the Central Suriname Nature Reserve or Raleighvallen.

Target market: [European / US / Dutch diaspora / regional travellers].
Current capacity: [number of beds / tours per year].
Access route: [Zanderij Airport → small aircraft / boat / road to Atjoni].
Key assets: [Voltzberg granite dome / harpie eagles / tapir / Saramaka culture].
Local community involvement: [Saramaka villages / guides / crafts].
Sustainability certification status: [none / in process / certified].
Budget for marketing and product development: [SRD/USD amount].
Competing destinations: [Guyana Rupununi / French Guiana / Amazon Brazil].

Create:
- differentiated eco-tourism product packages (birding, wildlife, cultural, adventure),
- pricing strategy in USD benchmarked against Guyana and Costa Rica lodges,
- digital marketing strategy (Instagram, Facebook, Dutch-language content, nature travel platforms),
- Saramaka community partnership and benefit-sharing framework,
- sustainability and low-impact operations guide (solar, waste, water),
- travel trade partnerships (Dutch tour operators, UK/US nature travel specialists),
- press and influencer outreach plan for international nature media,
- and grant funding strategy (IDB / GEF / Green Climate Fund for eco-tourism infrastructure).
```
**Benefits / Beneficios**: Diversifies Suriname's export earnings, creates income for interior Maroon and indigenous communities, and builds international brand recognition for Suriname's exceptional biodiversity.
