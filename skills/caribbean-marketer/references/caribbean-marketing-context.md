## Consumer Behaviour by Island

### Jamaica
- **Values**: Authenticity, quality, Jamaican pride, value for money
- **Decision drivers**: Word-of-mouth (massive), social proof, community reputation
- **Purchase channels**: Physical stores still dominant; WhatsApp commerce growing fast
- **Price sensitivity**: High for everyday goods; willing to pay premium for quality and brand story
- **Trust signals**: Known faces, community endorsements, longevity ("they've been there for years")
- **Marketing taboos**: Avoid anything that feels "foreign" or dismissive of Jamaican culture

### Trinidad & Tobago
- **Values**: Fete culture, food culture, community, ambition, innovation
- **Decision drivers**: Recommendation from friends/family, Instagram discovery, WhatsApp word-of-mouth
- **Carnival window**: February/March Carnival is the biggest commercial period — plan 3 months ahead
- **Food obsession**: Trinidadian food culture is globally renowned — food businesses have authentic storytelling gold
- **Trust signals**: "Trini-owned", community presence, family business history
- **Marketing taboos**: Never mock or trivialise Carnival, Divali, or cultural celebrations

### Barbados
- **Values**: Sophistication, quality, Bajan pride, excellence, tradition
- **Decision drivers**: Quality over price (more so than other islands), brand reputation, word-of-mouth
- **UK connection**: Strong British cultural influence — marketing can reference UK quality standards
- **Crop Over window**: July–August Crop Over festival is the biggest marketing window
- **Trust signals**: Established brand, professional presentation, Bajan-made emphasis
- **Marketing taboos**: Don't be too casual or "loud" — Bajan sensibility is more measured

### Guyana
- **Values**: Progress, ambition, multi-ethnic pride, oil boom optimism
- **Changing fast**: Oil revenue since 2020 is transforming the consumer landscape rapidly
- **WhatsApp dominant**: Primary consumer communication channel
- **Diaspora connection**: Large diaspora in USA and Canada — diaspora market is significant
- **Trust signals**: Local business, community reputation, WhatsApp visibility

## Platform-Specific Caribbean Marketing Guide

### WhatsApp Business — The #1 Tool

**Setup essentials**:
- Business name, category, and description filled out completely
- Business hours set
- Greeting message enabled
- Away message for outside business hours
- Quick replies for 5 most common questions
- Catalogue updated with products/services and prices

**Broadcast best practices**:
- Max 256 recipients per broadcast (WhatsApp limit)
- Only send to people who have your number saved
- 2–3 broadcasts per week maximum
- Personalise with name when possible
- Always include a CTA
- Include emojis — they increase open and response rates

**WhatsApp Status strategy**:
- Post to Status daily (product photos, behind-the-scenes, tips)
- Status disappears after 24 hours — perfect for limited offers
- Tap-through rate is very high — Caribbean users check Status frequently

### Instagram — The Visual Showcase

**Algorithm keys for Caribbean accounts**:
- Reels get 2–3x organic reach vs. static posts
- Post 3–5 times per week for consistent growth
- Stories daily — shows active presence
- Reply to every comment within 1 hour of posting (boosts distribution)
- Use all 10 hashtag slots

**Caribbean content that performs**:
- Food photography (flatlay or overhead with natural light)
- Behind-the-scenes of production or service
- Before/after (transformation, renovation, preparation)
- Cultural tie-in (referencing a local event or tradition)
- Customer testimonial videos (authentic, not scripted)
- Island scenery as backdrop for products

### Facebook — Community and Commerce

**Caribbean Facebook landscape**:
- Facebook Groups are extremely active in the Caribbean
- Local "Buy and Sell" groups, neighbourhood groups, community groups
- Business pages still relevant — many Caribbean consumers check Facebook pages before WhatsApp
- Facebook Events: critical for promotion of in-person events
- Facebook Marketplace: Growing for product sales

**Strategy for Caribbean SMEs**:
- Join and actively participate in relevant local Facebook Groups
- Post value content (not just promotions) in groups
- Create a Facebook Event for every significant business event or launch
- Run Facebook/Instagram Ads together via Meta Business Suite

## Caribbean Advertising Copy Principles

### Language Register
- **Caribbean English is valid**: "Come check us out", "Nuff deals available", "Sweet vibes guaranteed"
- **Code-switching works**: Mix standard English with local dialect strategically
- **Patois/Creole use**: Use sparingly and only if you know the dialect authentically
- **Spanish for Dominican Republic/Latin Caribbean**: Always use for primary markets

### Emotional Drivers in Caribbean Copy
1. **Pride**: "Caribbean-made", "Island-crafted", "Authentic [Island] flavour"
2. **Family**: Caribbean culture is family-centred — family benefits resonate
3. **Community**: "Supporting local", "Keeping the money in the community"
4. **Aspiration**: Caribbean consumers aspire to quality and status
5. **Joy**: Food, music, celebration — Caribbean marketing should feel joyful
6. **Nostalgia**: "Like grandma used to make", "Old-time [Island] recipe"
