## Regional Healthcare Context

The Caribbean and Latin America face unique healthcare challenges:
- High burden of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs): diabetes, hypertension, obesity
- HIV, dengue, zika, chikungunya - tropical disease burden
- Limited specialist availability in small island states
- Brain drain of medical professionals
- Underfunded public health systems
- Strong private healthcare growing in major cities
- Cuba's world-class medical system as regional model

## 50+ Healthcare AI Use Cases

### Clinical Decision Support
```
Prompt: "I am a [doctor/nurse practitioner] in [country].
Patient: [age, sex, weight, height].
Chief complaint: [describe].
Vital signs: BP [X/X], HR [X], Temp [X], SpO2 [X]%.
Relevant history: [chronic conditions, medications].
Current medications: [list].
Lab results (if available): [results].

In the context of common conditions in [country/Caribbean region],
provide: differential diagnoses (most to least likely),
recommended investigations, initial management approach,
red flag signs to watch for, and referral criteria.
Note: this supports clinical decision-making, not replaces physician judgment."
```

### Patient Education
```
Prompt: "Create patient education materials about [condition: Type 2 Diabetes/
Hypertension/HIV/Dengue/Maternal health] for patients in [country].
Patient literacy level: [basic/standard].
Language: [English/Spanish/French/Haitian Creole/Papiamento].
Format: [single page fact sheet/WhatsApp message series/poster].

Include: what the condition is (simple terms),
local risk factors and statistics for [country],
symptoms to watch for, when to seek care,
diet advice using LOCALLY AVAILABLE foods in [country],
lifestyle changes practical for [country] context,
available medications and where to get them in [country],
and local support resources."
```

### Hospital Administration
```
Prompt: "Create [document type: discharge summary/referral letter/
case notes template/triage protocol] for a [type of facility] in [country].
Patient/clinical context: [describe].
Required format: [Ministry of Health standard/regional standard].
Language: [language].
Ensure compliance with [country's health records regulations].
Keep clinical language at [appropriate level for recipient]."
```

### Telemedicine for Small Island States
```
Prompt: "Design a telemedicine program for [country/territory]
to connect patients with specialists in [specialty].
Current situation: [patients travel to Trinidad/Barbados/US for care].
Technology available: [smartphone penetration X%, internet quality].
Partner institutions: [list potential specialist partners].
Target conditions: [most common referral diagnoses].
Create: patient eligibility criteria, consultation workflow,
technology requirements and alternatives for low-bandwidth,
data privacy compliance framework, billing model,
and expected impact on medical travel costs."
```

### Public Health Campaigns
```
Prompt: "Create a public health campaign for [country/region]
targeting [disease/issue: NCDs/HIV/maternal mortality/vaccine hesitancy].
Target population: [demographics].
Key message: [message].
Channels available: [TV/radio/social media/community health workers].
Language: [language, include Creole/Patois variants if needed].
Cultural considerations: [specific to country].
Create: campaign concept, key messages for each channel,
radio script (60 seconds), social media content (5 posts),
community health worker talking points,
and success metrics."
```

## AI Healthcare Ethics in Our Region

**Key considerations:**
1. Always position AI as clinical support, not replacement
2. Ensure AI models are tested on diverse (including Afro-Caribbean, indigenous) populations
3. Protect patient data per national laws (Jamaica Data Protection Act, LGPD Brazil, etc.)
4. Address algorithmic bias in diagnostic AI trained on non-representative data
5. Ensure AI-generated health content is reviewed by qualified professionals
6. Consider low-literacy populations and design inclusive interfaces
